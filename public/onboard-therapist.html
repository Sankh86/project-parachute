We are looking for local community and private practice therapists who would like to support medical staff in the hospitals working on the front lines of the COVID-19 pandemic.

We know that times of uncertainty and chronic stress can exacerbate existing mental health concerns. Please consider supporting this effort. If you have the ability to provide pro bono online therapy services, please complete the form below.

Project Parachute provides a matching service only. Therapists are expected to have a valid license in the state in which they practice (or practice under a licensed supervisor), their own liability insurance, and their own consent forms. Eleos can provide a HIPAA compliant server for teletherapy (click here).

Who is eligible to sign-up?

  • Fully licensed therapists
  • Provisionally licensed therapists (you have a temporary or a training license but are still supervised)

Unfortunately, therapists-in-training (graduate students, interns, etc.) are not able to provide services through Project Parachute. If you are interested in other volunteer opportunities, please email us at projectparachute.eleos@gmail.com.

Looking to update your profile information?

If you would like to make any changes to your profile, please submit the form here here and allow us 24 hours to update it. We are currently working on the login functionality for therapists and will notify you when it’s available.

Questions? See our FAQ page or send us an email.

Please discuss providing pro-bono therapy with your supervisor before signing up to help. We also recommend you consult with your liability insurance about being able to conduct pro-bono work.

Do you have liability/malpractice insurance?

Unfortunately, we are unable to list your profile if you don’t have liability insurance. Project Parachute provides matching service only and we don’t provide liability coverage.

Project Parachute provides a place where COVID-19 frontliners can find therapists who are interested in providing pro bono therapy to frontliners. However, Project Parachute isn't company like Better Help or Talkspace. We aren't hiring employees and therefore, we don't provide consent forms or malpractice insurance. We're more like Open Path Collective website where you advertise that you want to give back to COVID-19 frontliners in your state by offering pro bono therapy. When a COVID-19 frontliner matches with you for care, you would take them into your usual practice.

How does matching work?

  • Healthcare frontliners will be able to browse therapists in their state and select one they want to work with. Once we receive a request from a frontliner, we will notify you by email with their information. It will be up to you to contact them and set up an appointment. Clients will be notified that the appointment is not confirmed until they hear from you.
  • For administrative purposes, you can treat the frontliner as another patient in your existing practice (i.e., consent forms, handling no-shows, etc.). It is completely up to you how many free sessions you offer, how often you see the client, and how long the sessions are. Please set these expectations with the client at the beginning of therapy.
  • It may be a month or longer before we are able to make a match. We're hearing from frontliners that they definitely want someone to talk to, but that they also feel too overwhelmed to seek out support at this moment. We're anticipating a second mental health wave after this immediate crisis.

How many clients will I get? You will continue receiving matches as long as your profile is active on our website. If you are no longer available to provide services, please request for your profile to be hidden or removed by emailing us at projectparachute.eleos@gmail.com. You can re-activate your profile at any time.

Who counts as a frontliner:

Someone who is working in a face-to-face healthcare setting where they may or may not have direct contact and/or care for someone with COVID-19. This includes healthcare workers and also admin staff (e.g. check in), cleaners, cafeteria workers, etc.

Someone who is working in a face-to-face public health setting (with homeless individuals, at COVID testing centers) as someone who is promoting screening, testing, and care for those with COVID-19.

Mental health providers who are providing care to the frontline as part of their job in a healthcare setting. Often, mental health faculty or staff cannot get care from their peers as they provide mental health care to frontliners in their own hospital. By supporting those who are helping others provide mental health support, we can amplify our efforts.

Keep in mind. Humans are complicated. Someone might be working on the frontline but also be struggling in another area at home or with pre-existing mental health condition that they want to talk to you about first.
