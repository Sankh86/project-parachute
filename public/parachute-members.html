We can't keep anyone from hitting the ground but therapy, like a parachute, can soften your landing.

We define a healthcare frontliner as someone who is...

  • In a face-to-face healthcare setting where they may or may not have direct contact and/or care for someone with COVID-19. This includes healthcare workers and also administrative staff (e.g. check in), cleaners, cafeteria workers, etc.
  • In a face-to-face public health setting (with homeless individuals, at COVID testing centers) to promote screening, testing, and care for those with COVID-19.
  • Providing mental health services to the frontline as part of their job in a healthcare setting (not exclusively over telehealth).

Do you believe you qualify for our services for frontline health-care workers according to the definition above?

Thank you for your interest in receiving services through Project Parachute. Unfortunately, we are unable to provide services to people who do not fit our narrow definition of a COVID-19 frontliner.

We’ve included some free or low-fee options for individual care that may be helpful as you look for care. If you believe that this decision has been made in error or you have any further questions, please email us at projectparachute.eleos@gmail.com.
